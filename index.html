💡 Not sure where to start?

If I run a global business, what do I need to do — step by step?

Follow these steps and you will have the key legal protections in place for operating across Europe, the US, and China.

1

Understand what personal data you collect

List every type of information you collect about people — customers, employees, website visitors. This could be names, emails, location, payment info, or browsing habits.

🔍 Start with a Privacy Risk Check
2

Check if your data activities are high-risk

If you process large amounts of personal data, use tracking, profiling, or sensitive data (health, finances), run a quick Privacy Risk Check before proceeding.

🔐 Privacy Risk Check
3

Selling in or to China? Run a PIPIA

If you handle the personal data of people in China, China's PIPL law usually requires a formal Personal Information Protection Impact Assessment (PIPIA). Our bilingual tool walks you through it.

🛡️ China PIPIA Assessment
4

Set up agreements with your suppliers

Do you use third-party tools like Mailchimp, Stripe, or Salesforce that touch your customers' data? You need a written agreement with each of them.

📋 Supplier Data Agreement
5

Review contracts & NDAs before signing

Before signing any business contract or NDA, run it through our AI review. It flags risky clauses in plain language so you know what you are agreeing to.

📄 NDA & Contract Review
6

Handle data transfers across countries

Sending customer data from Europe to the US, China, or elsewhere? Each route has its own rules. We will tell you exactly what applies to your situation.

🌎 Cross-Border Data Check
7

Know what to do if something goes wrong

If there is ever a data breach, you may have only hours to notify the authorities. This tool walks you through who to notify, when, and what to say.

🚨 Incident Response Guide
